   Update! August 29, 2001
  • Shield proof-of-concept is complete. Now when I fire, the shield lights up... I still have to make it animate nicely...which should be doable but slightly tricky due to variable mesh sizes, etc.
  • I'm using OpenGL lights for shields, meaning that the lights themselves around the shield light it up for damage.
  • each shot that hits factors into the creation of possibly new lights to light up the shield in certain places (where in other cases it would be completely transparent).
  • this method of doing shields doesn't suffer from the problem of large ships getting hit in many places (just put lights on both sides) nor the problem of having to change and redownload the mesh each time part of the shield is hit (to change the way it looks). It just uses weak lights that fade to make the shield shine.
  • Expect the shields to look magnificent in the next release, which I anticipate fairly shortly.

   Update! August 12, 2001
  • BSP trees now work perfectly for both line and starship collisions.
  • this means that, unlike vegastrike classic, you'll be able to fly close to the hull of a large starship, hug the deck, fly through the launch bay...
  • Any closed model (eg Midway) can only be hit by a well placed beam (no hitting if you fire between the legs of those Midway pants)
  • This also will make way for skin-tight shield effects on those large ships!
  • an optimized BSP tree generator is available that can generate trees for Vegastrike and even for prophecy.
  • this generator can minimize the size of the tree or the time it takes to search through it, using ideas inspired from the crystal space engine (http://crystal.sourceforge.net)
  • if anyone has any ideas how to enforce the closed-ness of a model, please let me know. Currently many of the vegastrike classic models are "open" and have gaps in them that allow you to collide, mid-space! (bad things)
  • These BSP trees are the last _major_ challenge to getting vegastrike working.
  • from here on out it's all details associated with gameplay and organization, as opposed to new algorithms I need to learn.
