   Lies! April 2, 2002
  • Well As most of you have realized, yesterday was indeed the first, or as some like to know it... the day of the April Fools. I would like to thank those april fools ( you know who you are ) for participating and sending me pleas to be part of the beta. However, don't take that to mean that multiplayer is defunct... read on!
  • Let me clear up some misconceptions...as well as some conceptions
  • The good news: Features that will be in Vegastrike-0.2.2
  • Check out the new Starship Guide for vegastrike thanks to the excellent Marcel Paré, who has contributed numerous starships for the 0.2.2 release.
  • Multiplayer is indeed in the vegastrike development tree. Since I was very small I have always longed for the split-screen version of multiplayer the most, and only one game of mine had it: Wing Commander Armada. That way I could play games with friends even though we had only one computer. Finally I am bringing a new splitscreen version of Vegastrike. You and your friends can cooperate by flying each others turrets or working together to complete a mission. Or you can Attack each other, sparring for superiority .
  • I've added trading modes, and you can accept missions from within bases.
  • Cargo prices fluctuate as other privateers trade in their goods.
  • You can use money from cargo to purchase new weapons for your ship or even a brand new, off-the-shelf brand from any of your favorite ship dealers.
  • Cargo missions have players run contraband through areas (don't get searched) and have players escort cargo ships to their destinations.
  • Bounty hunters will be gleeful at the bounty missions that require the player to hunt down a well armed foe...for a price.
  • Damages causes systems to short out and consoles to burst. Weapons blow off and any of your systems can fail at any time. Don't let this discourage you: you can always buy a repair bot!
  • Communications are supported: they let you insult a foe or carry on a winding conversation with a friend. Just don't let your tounge go off or you may have more than one enemy.
  • Both video and auditory communications are supported. Unfortunately we have no voice actors nor anything for potential voice actors to say. If you think you can help script some conversations please mail us (see link below).
  • The bad news
  • Neither TCP/IP nor IPX will be supported until some Networking programmers join our team. (that will be the day) If you are an enthusiastic networking programmer that is willing to learn new code, we urge you to join our team.
  • The release has also been delayed due to lack of basic datafiles such as cargo and contraband lists. If you think you can help out with prices and types of purchasable cargo and possible weapons upgrades, please contact us , and I can help you get started.

Update! January 22, 2002
  • Work on Vegastrike has been marching forward. Alex has made major progress on a mission scripting system so powerful that it can be used to write entire AI modules and make starships warp in, etc all in one package. It should be relatively simple for veteran C programmers to adapt to this system of scripting.
  • I have rewritten the collision system to use crystal space 's RAPID collision system. Instead of seeing if the mesh is inside, it only checks against the skin of the starship. This means it checks against each and every polygon of each mesh, making for very accuract capship to capship or fighter to fighter collisions. The most beautiful thing is that when crystal space modifies its collision system, I can update my own with their source since I copied their GPL-licensed code directly instead of forking and making vegastrike compatible. The code is a little longer, but it's just as fast.
  • I have completely redone planets so that the terrain stays flush with the planet surface. It's amazing to fly across the planet and see the sunrise on the land! It was challenging to get vehicles to drive on this terrain, and I believe that currently vehicles are rather hacked into the system--but at least they work.
  • I have added some fog spheres so that in certain areas fog is triggered.
  • Daniel Aleksandrow has worked on adding some nice backgrounds

  • Reliant has put together a really cool ship builder that will let future privateers build their own starships out of the component pieces. Go Reliant!
  • Png support has been added so that vegastrike accepts png as well as bitmap files. They load up about three times as fast as the .bmps.
  • Time compression has been added to let users speed up or decrease the time (for autopilot-like maneuvers)
  • Rotation has been added for orbital objects around their own axis...right now it's global...but soon it will be on a per-planet basis
  • A text output display has been hacked in by Alex...and an elite style radar also has been added to the cockpit displays.

Update! August 29, 2001
  • Shield proof-of-concept is complete. Now when I fire, the shield lights up... I still have to make it animate nicely...which should be doable but slightly tricky due to variable mesh sizes, etc.
  • I'm using OpenGL lights for shields, meaning that the lights themselves around the shield light it up for damage.
  • each shot that hits factors into the creation of possibly new lights to light up the shield in certain places (where in other cases it would be completely transparent).
  • this method of doing shields doesn't suffer from the problem of large ships getting hit in many places (just put lights on both sides) nor the problem of having to change and redownload the mesh each time part of the shield is hit (to change the way it looks). It just uses weak lights that fade to make the shield shine .
  • Expect the shields to look magnificent in the next release, which I anticipate fairly shortly.

Update! August 12, 2001
  • BSP trees now work perfectly for both line and starship collisions.
  • this means that, unlike vegastrike classic, you'll be able to fly close to the hull of a large starship, hug the deck, fly through the launch bay...
  • Any closed model (eg Midway) can only be hit by a well placed beam (no hitting if you fire between the legs of those Midway pants)
  • This also will make way for skin-tight shield effects on those large ships!
  • an optimized BSP tree generator is available that can generate trees for Vegastrike and even for prophecy.
  • this generator can minimize the size of the tree or the time it takes to search through it, using ideas inspired from the crystal space engine (http://crystal.sourceforge.net)
  • if anyone has any ideas how to enforce the closed-ness of a model, please let me know. Currently many of the vegastrike classic models are "open" and have gaps in them that allow you to collide, mid-space! (bad things)
  • These BSP trees are the last _major_ challenge to getting vegastrike working.
  • from here on out it's all details associated with gameplay and organization, as opposed to new algorithms I need to learn.
