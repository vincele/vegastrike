Editing Weapons Using Texturiser

Specific Controls:

"[" or "]" "y" or "u" "h" "j" "k" "l" ; or '
Resize logo. Rotate logo. Turn logo into gun. Turn logo into engine. * Turn logo into turret. Turn logo into lights. Travel through active logos.

"s" "a" 1-4 5-8 "o" "p"
Add logo. Delete logo. Pulls logo towards current vertex. Repells logo from current vertex. Makes force logo. Makes squadron logo.
* Not yet implemented.

Hit space to change to weapon mode. The texture will disappear.

Weapon mode is a bit more complicated than texturing, any given weapon must be on the surface of your starship.

Also you may position painted decals on your starship. This is used for force identification (not yet used in the game) and will be eventually used to identify captured ships in future multiplayer.

The hornet so far has 2 confed logos, 2 engines and no weapons.

Try resizing the current logo by pressing "[" or "]" to make it bigger or smaller and spin it left or right be pressing "y" or "u."

You can change the logos into weapons by pressing "h" for gun mounts. The particular weapon can be selected later.

Similarly, they are made into turrets through "k" and lights through "l." It is probably best to make them different sizes so you don't get confused with thich one is what.

Try adding a new weapon or logo, press "s". To position it on the current polygon, press a number 1 through 4 to draw it towards the selected vertex. Similarly 5-8 pushes it away from that vertex. This can be used relaticely easily to position polygons on mesh.

Now unfortunately the saving of the weapons is somewhat user unfriendly. when you exit and save, the results go into a file known as export-unit.xunit

This is the editor's best guess at what you'd like to save. It names all newly selected weapons as lasers and leaves all old weapons there too. If you move weapons around or even edit them, it may have duplicates of them. You must edit export-unit.xunit by hand to delete the (possible) duplicates and to specify mount type and weapon type. To see how to edit the unit file fully, see the unit section in the howto.
