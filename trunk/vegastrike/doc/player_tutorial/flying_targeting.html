Flying:

Currently, you can steer your ship via keyboard or joystick - joystick is of course recommended.

You can use the following keys to steer:

  • cursor arrow keys (or joystick)

    • banks your starship up,down,left or right
  • insert and delete

    • rotates your ship around it's forward axis
  • TAB

    • fire the afterburner
    you have to keep TAB presses to use the afterburner
  • + and -

    • increase or decrease speed
  • backspace

    • full stop
  • y

    • matches the speed of the targeted ship
  • ` or capslock

    • shelton key - you can do the shelton slide with it
  • A

    • autopilot: flies your ship to your target (usually a base or station or jumppoint)
Use + and - to increase/decrease your speed while flying around

But if you really wanna go fast, press TAB to put in the afterburners to get near to an enemy fighter

If you press y then, your speed will be matched with the targets speed, and you can easily fire on it

Newtonian Physics:

Vegastrike usel real, newtonian physics and forces to calculate where the space ships are flying too.

This means, that when your ship has built up a certain velocity, it will continue to fly in this direction even when you rotate/bank the ship around.
To fly in a new direction, you have to accellerate the ship into that direction.

Targeting:

You can fire (and hit) on any target you want - even if you're not targeting the ship you're firing at.

But it is advised that you target the ship you want to fire on.

The targeted ship will be highlighted and it's blip on the radar will appear bigger, so you'll always know here your target is.
A rectangular target box will appear round the target.
This target box will have the same color as the blip on the radar - green for friends, red for enemies, yellow for neutral ships

If you want that all units in space should have a box rendered around them - so that you can see them better - you have to edit vegastrike.config:

  • section "graphics", subsection "hud", variable "drawAllTargetBoxes"
  • set this variable to "true" to have boxes on all units
  • in this mode, friends will be green, enemies red, neutral ships yellow and your target will be purple
: 
 
  • The left shows a screenshot with drawAllTargetBoxes=true

    • you can easily identity your current target (purple) and who's friend and who's hostile
  • the right screenshot was taken with drawAllTargetBoxes=false

    • your targeted ship has a red box - it's hostile
Use those keys to target differently:
  • t - cycle Targets

    • press t to cycle between targets
    all possible targets (enemies, friends, planets, jumppoints, stations etc) are cycled
  • p - Pick Target

    • selects the target that is currently the nearest to your crosshairs
    If you want to pick a special target, steer your ship until the crosshair is on it, and then press p
  • n - nearest Target

    • selects the nearest target for you
  • h - select Threat

    • select the enemy target that is most threatening to you

Some ships have got an ITTS display to help you in aiming at your target.

The above screenshots shows two rectangles - the left, smaller one is the ITTS indicator, the big to the right is the target box that is drawn around your target.

The ITTS calculates the point in space where you have to aim/fire at to hit your target, when it keeps flying with that speed and direction.

So, you should always aim at your ITTS, if your ship has one.
